# The Argent Order

## Leadership Training System

Version: 1.0

Status: Canonical Human Multiplication Engine

---

# Purpose

This system defines how leaders are created.

Not appointed.

Not assumed.

Not self-declared.

Created through structured pressure, responsibility, and evaluation.

---

# CORE PRINCIPLE

```text id="core"
Leadership is not a title.
It is a proven behavioural pattern under responsibility.
```

---

# LEADERSHIP PIPELINE

All members move through the same pipeline:

```text id="pipeline"
Initiate

↓

Brother

↓

Veteran

↓

Captain

↓

Officer

↓

Mentor

↓

Steward
```

---

# 1. BROTHER → VETERAN

## Objective

Stabilise discipline.

---

## Requirements:

* consistent Rule of Life adherence
* campaign completion
* basic pod participation

---

## Transition Rule:

```text id="brother_vet"
Consistency over time = promotion
```

---

# 2. VETERAN → CAPTAIN

## Objective

Prove reliability under responsibility.

---

## Requirements:

* strong discipline consistency
* active participation in pod
* ability to support others

---

## Evaluation:

* shows up without reminders
* completes commitments
* begins influencing peers positively

---

## Output:

Eligible for pod leadership.

---

# 3. CAPTAIN → OFFICER

## Objective

Scale leadership beyond one pod.

---

## Requirements:

* successful pod management
* stable attendance enforcement
* conflict handling ability
* accountability discipline

---

## Responsibilities added:

* multiple pods oversight
* quality control
* escalation handling

---

# 4. OFFICER → MENTOR

## Objective

Move from enforcement to formation.

---

## Requirements:

* leadership maturity
* emotional stability under pressure
* ability to correct behaviour without control issues

---

## Focus shift:

From:

* managing behaviour

To:

* forming identity

---

## Responsibilities:

* training Captains
* improving pod quality
* refining formation depth

---

# 5. MENTOR → STEWARD

## Objective

System-level responsibility.

---

## Requirements:

* long-term leadership consistency
* system thinking ability
* alignment with mission integrity

---

## Role:

* protect doctrine
* guide system evolution
* oversee leadership pipeline health

---

# LEADERSHIP EVALUATION MODEL

Leadership is evaluated across 5 dimensions:

---

## 1. CONSISTENCY

Do they show up without external pressure?

---

## 2. RESPONSIBILITY

Do they take ownership of outcomes?

---

## 3. DISCIPLINE

Do they live the system they enforce?

---

## 4. INFLUENCE

Do others improve around them?

---

## 5. STABILITY

Do they remain effective under stress?

---

# PROMOTION RULE

```text id="promotion"
Behaviour confirms identity before rank changes
```

Ranks follow behaviour.

Never the reverse.

---

# LEADERSHIP FAILURE MODES

---

## 1. AUTHORITY WITHOUT DISCIPLINE

Leaders who enforce rules they do not follow.

Fix:
Immediate demotion or retraining.

---

## 2. SOFT LEADERSHIP

Avoidance of correction.

Fix:
Structured accountability training.

---

## 3. CONTROL BEHAVIOUR

Over-managing members.

Fix:
Shift to formation-based leadership.

---

## 4. INCONSISTENCY

Unreliable leadership presence.

Fix:
Return to Veteran level.

---

# TRAINING SYSTEM

Leadership is trained through:

---

## 1. OBSERVATION PHASE

* shadowing higher leaders
* attending leadership calls

---

## 2. CONTROLLED RESPONSIBILITY

* managing small pods
* limited scope authority

---

## 3. FULL RESPONSIBILITY

* independent pod leadership
* accountability enforcement

---

## 4. SYSTEM OVERSIGHT

* multi-pod management
* training others

---

The Argent Order does not assign leaders.
