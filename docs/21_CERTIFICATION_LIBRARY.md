# The Argent Order

## Certification Library

Version: 1.0

Status: Canonical Certification & Progression Framework

---

# Purpose

Certifications exist to recognize demonstrated competence, reliability, and service.

They are not rewards for participation.

They are not attendance trophies.

They are evidence that a man has consistently demonstrated a particular standard.

---

# Core Principles

## Principle 1

Responsibility before recognition.

A title is earned through action.

---

## Principle 2

Character before competence.

Skill without virtue is insufficient.

---

## Principle 3

Certifications unlock opportunities to serve.

Not opportunities to boast.

---

## Principle 4

Certifications can be revoked.

Failure to maintain standards may result in suspension or removal.

---

# Progression Framework

Community progression follows two parallel tracks.

## Rank Track

Represents trust and responsibility.

```text
Initiate

↓

Brother

↓

Veteran

↓

Captain

↓

Officer

↓

Mentor

↓

Steward
```

---

## Certification Track

Represents demonstrated capability.

```text
Formation

Builder

Brotherhood

Leadership

Mentorship

Specialist
```

A man may hold multiple certifications.

---

# RANK SYSTEM

---

## Initiate

Entry Rank.

Requirements:

* Joined community
* Accepted rules
* Completed onboarding
* Connected Discord and Portal

Permissions:

* Access basic channels
* Join campaigns

Responsibilities:

* Learn
* Participate
* Build consistency

---

## Brother

First earned rank.

Requirements:

* Foundation Campaign completed
* Rule Of Life established
* Minimum activity period
* Pod participation

Permissions:

* Full community access
* Eligible for certifications

Responsibilities:

* Accountability
* Consistency
* Contribution

---

## Veteran

Reliable member.

Requirements:

* Multiple campaigns completed
* Sustained activity
* Positive community contribution

Responsibilities:

* Model standards
* Assist newer members

---

## Captain

First leadership rank.

Requirements:

* Brotherhood Certification
* Leadership Certification
* Proven accountability

Responsibilities:

* Lead pods
* Track member progress
* Resolve minor disputes

Permissions:

* Leadership dashboard
* Pod management tools

---

## Officer

Community operations leader.

Requirements:

* Long-term reliability
* Leadership track completion
* Steward recommendation

Responsibilities:

* Community management
* Moderation
* Growth support

Permissions:

* Administrative systems
* Community oversight

---

## Mentor

Formation leader.

Requirements:

* Mentor Certification
* Demonstrated maturity
* Leadership approval

Responsibilities:

* Guide members
* Conduct reviews
* Formation support

---

## Steward

Highest operational rank.

Requirements:

* Exceptional character
* Proven leadership
* Long-term service

Responsibilities:

* Protect mission
* Protect culture
* Develop future leaders

---

# CERTIFICATION SYSTEM

---

# FORMATION CERTIFICATION

Purpose:

Demonstrate foundational Catholic formation.

Requirements:

* Foundation Campaign
* Prayer Campaign
* Active Rule Of Life
* Weekly reviews completed

Assessment:

* Reflection submission
* Accountability verification

Unlocks:

* Advanced campaigns

---

# DISCIPLINE CERTIFICATION

Purpose:

Demonstrate personal consistency.

Requirements:

* Discipline Campaign
* Streak requirements
* Rule compliance benchmarks

Assessment:

* Performance review
* Accountability verification

Unlocks:

* Leadership pathway

---

# BROTHERHOOD CERTIFICATION

Purpose:

Demonstrate accountability and service.

Requirements:

* Pod participation
* Meeting attendance
* Peer endorsements

Assessment:

* Pod review

Unlocks:

* Captain pathway

---

# CERTIFICATION LEVELS

Every certification can have levels.

Example:

```text
Builder I

Builder II

Builder III

Master Builder
```

---

Example:

```text
Leadership I

Leadership II

Leadership III
```

This creates progression without unnecessary rank inflation.

# FINAL PRINCIPLE

Ranks indicate trust.

Certifications indicate competence.

Neither exists for status.

Both exist to support formation, service, responsibility, and mission.

The highest achievement in The Argent Order is not recognition.

It is becoming a man who can be trusted with responsibility and who consistently serves God, family, community, and society.
